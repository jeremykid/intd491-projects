## Problem

Standard map apps often stop being useful once users enter a building or need route guidance based on landmarks, door types, elevators, or temporary construction barriers.

## Solution

The project supplements conventional routing with the kind of cues students and visitors actually rely on: visual landmarks, threshold notes, elevator locations, and confidence markers at each route segment.

## Evaluation

User testing focused on first-time campus visitors. Participants completed wayfinding tasks with fewer hesitations when the route instructions referenced recognizable landmarks instead of room numbers alone.
